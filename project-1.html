Design Tool Web App

Summary

At Sidebench, I played a pivotal role in the development of a cutting-edge Design Tool Web App. This innovative application was crafted with the intention of optimizing the sales lead process for a leading iron and steel manufacturing company, renowned for their expertise in crafting fences, gates, doors, and more. As this forward-thinking company began its expansion into the residential sector, their primary objective was to revolutionize the sales process by enabling customers to effortlessly bring their visions to life.

This was achieved through a sophisticated Design Tool Web App, empowering customers to seamlessly integrate product overlays onto images of their homes, ultimately resulting in a significant reduction of salesperson hours.

My Role

Product Design: Employed strategic messaging and user-focused language to compose compelling marketing copy, delineate key selling points, and outline the user journey.

Backlog Management: Spearheaded the development team through backlog grooming, sprint planning, retrospectives, product demonstrations, and quality assurance, ensuring high-quality results. Composed user stories, established acceptance criteria, and created clearly defined tickets in Jira.

Results

Reducing salesperson workload: The web app will enable LVIS to streamline the sales process by providing customers with an intuitive tool to create initial designs and then connect with salespeople to finalize their projects.

Boosted lead generation: The integration with Pipedrive will help LVIS maintain an organized customer base, allowing for better lead generation and management.

Improved administrative oversight: Administrators will have the necessary tools to oversee salespeople accounts and monitor project statuses, ensuring efficient management of the sales process.

Enhanced customization and visualization: Customers will be able to upload a photo of their home, experiment with different product overlays, and customize their designs by color, shape, features, and more. This will enable them and the salesperson to visualize the final product before proceeding with construction.
